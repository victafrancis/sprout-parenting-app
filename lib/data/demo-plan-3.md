# 🌱 Weekly Developmental Plan  
**Age:** 11 months  
**Primary Focus Schema:** Enveloping & In-and-Out (Pulling tissues/scarves, hiding under blankets, container play)  
**Secondary Focus:** Orientation (Looking through Tupperware/scarves, dropping and tracking)  
**Routine Focus:** Feeding/Clean-up & Dressing

---

## 📊 Logic for this Week's Plan
Yumi is having a wonderful explosion of cognitive and motor skills! She is confidently pulling to stand, balancing for up to 10 seconds, and cruising. Her recent play shows a deep fascination with the **Enveloping and In-and-Out schemas**—she loves pulling tissues and scarves from boxes and playing peek-a-boo under blankets. She is also experimenting with **Orientation** by looking at the world through see-through scarves and Tupperware lids. 

Recently, she showed some frustration when trying to put large stuffed toys into a small tissue box hole, and when trying to rotate her wrist for a lid-slit puzzle. This week, we will lower the frustration by providing containers with *wide* openings to help her master the "putting back in" phase of container play. We will also lean into her beautiful new communication skills (pointing while saying "aaahh") and her interest in practical life (wiping the table!).

---

## 🎯 Weekly Goals (Observable)

**Goal 1 – Fine Motor & Spatial Reasoning**  
Attempts to place a small object back into a wide-mouth container (without frustration) on at least 3 days this week.

**Goal 2 – Communication & Joint Attention**  
Points to an object or picture in a book and makes a vocalization (e.g., "aaahh") while making eye contact with the caregiver to share interest, at least once per day.

**Goal 3 – Gross Motor Balance**  
Stands independently for 10+ seconds while engaged with a toy placed on a low surface, on at least 4 days this week.

---

## 🧺 Weekly Activity Menu

Short (2–10 minute), flexible activity options. Repetition is highly encouraged—if Yumi loves an activity, let her do it every day!

### Monday
**1. Wide-Mouth Scarf Stuffing (Enveloping / In-and-Out)**  
- **Materials:** A large mixing bowl or wide basket, and her favorite play scarves.  
- **Adult prompt:** "Look at all the scarves!" Let her pull them out. Then model loosely dropping one back in. "Can you put it in?"  
- **Extend/Progress option:** If she easily puts them in the wide bowl, offer a container with a slightly smaller opening (but still much larger than a tissue box).

**2. See-Through Peek-a-Boo (Orientation)**  
- **Materials:** A sheer, see-through scarf or a clear Tupperware lid.  
- **Adult prompt:** Drape the scarf over your head or hers. Ask, "Where's Yumi?" Wait for her to pull it down, then smile broadly: "There she is!"  
- **Extend/Progress option:** Hold the Tupperware lid up to your face and make silly faces through the plastic.

**3. Single-Toy Book Time (Communication)**  
- **Materials:** One picture book featuring animals (cats, chicks, etc.).  
- **Adult prompt:** Sit together on the floor. Wait for her to point and say "aaahh." Respond enthusiastically: "Yes! That's a cat. Meow!"  

### Tuesday
**1. Velcro Fruit Chop (Connecting)**  
- **Materials:** Velcro play food.  
- **Adult prompt:** Hand her a connected piece of fruit. "Can you pull it apart?" Celebrate when she uses her strength to separate them.  
- **Extend option:** Stick the velcro pieces lightly to a piece of felt or a couch cushion and let her pull them off.

**2. High Chair Drop & Track (Trajectory / Orientation)**  
- **Materials:** 3-4 safe, soft objects (a block, a rolled-up sock, a soft ball).  
- **Adult prompt:** While she is in her high chair, hand her one item at a time. When she drops it, look down with her. "Whoa, it went down!"  

**3. Table Wipe Down (Practical Life Routine)**  
- **Context:** Right after a meal.  
- **Prompt:** Hand her a clean wipe or a water-dampened cloth. "Thank you for helping clean!" Let her mimic your left-and-right wiping motions on her tray or the table.

### Wednesday
**1. Layered Hide-and-Seek (Object Permanence)**  
- **Materials:** A musical toy, a small box or pillow, and a blanket.  
- **Adult prompt:** Turn the music on. Hide the toy under the pillow, then put the blanket over it. "Where is the music?" Let her dig through the layers to find it.  

**2. Lego Pull-Apart (Connecting)**  
- **Materials:** A few large, interlocking Lego blocks.  
- **Adult prompt:** Connect two blocks firmly. Hand them to Yumi and let her use her bilateral coordination to pull them apart.  

**3. The Standing Reach Game (Gross Motor)**  
- **Materials:** A highly desired toy (like a favorite book or a new Tupperware lid).  
- **Adult prompt:** Place the toy on a low coffee table or couch cushion where she must pull to stand to reach it. Stand close by to spot her as she balances for those 10 seconds!  

### Thursday
**1. Big Box, Small Toys (Spatial Relationships)**  
- **Materials:** A large cardboard box (flaps removed) and a few small (but safe/non-choking) toys.  
- **Adult prompt:** This reverses her tissue-box frustration. Give her small items to drop into a massive opening. This guarantees success and builds her confidence in "putting things back."  

**2. Cruising Delivery (Transporting)**  
- **Materials:** A toy she can hold in one hand.  
- **Adult prompt:** Have her hold a toy while cruising along the couch, or while holding your hand to walk. "Let's take the block to the chair!"  

**3. Choice-Based Book Interaction (Communication)**  
- **Prompt strategy:** Hold up two different books. Ask, "Which book today?" Pause and wait for her to point with her index finger or reach for the one she wants before handing it over.

### Friday
**1. Tissue Box Rescue (Enveloping)**  
- **Materials:** An empty tissue box stuffed with fabric scraps or dry wipes.  
- **Adult prompt:** Place it in front of her and let her pull them out one by one. (Don't worry about having her put them back in this box yet, just let her enjoy the extraction!).  

**2. Unbreakable Mirror Play (Orientation)**  
- **Materials:** A baby-safe floor mirror.  
- **Adult prompt:** Sit with her in front of the mirror. Put a scarf on your head and look in the mirror together. "Look at us!"  

**3. Boundary Redirection Game (Social Referencing)**  
- **Materials:** A safe, heavy object she *is* allowed to touch (like a large sealed water bottle), placed near where the humidifier or Stanley tumblers usually are.  
- **Adult prompt:** When she goes to explore, let her touch the safe object. If she reaches for a forbidden object, gently say "No, that is not for Yumi," and immediately offer the safe alternative. Acknowledge her laugh, but hold the boundary playfully.

## Weekend (Repeat & Extend)

Repeat her favorite activities from the week. 
Optional progression ideas:
- **Extend the Wipe Down:** Give her a dry cloth to wipe the low TV stand or a safe cabinet while you clean the living room.
- **Extend the Peek-a-Boo:** Play peek-a-boo by hiding *yourself* behind a door or couch and popping out, encouraging her to crawl and find you.
- **Extend the Container Play:** Introduce two different sized bowls and let her experiment with moving toys back and forth between them.

## 👶 Caregiving-as-Learning Scripts

### Routine 1 – Feeding & Clean-Up (Practical Life)
- **Script:** "All done eating! Now it's time to clean." (pause)  
- **Action:** Hand her a damp cloth.  
- **Prompt:** "Can you wipe the table?" (wait for her to start moving her hands left and right).  
- **Narrate:** "Wow, wipe, wipe, wipe. Thank you for helping!" 

### Routine 2 – Dressing (Enveloping / Peek-a-boo)
- **Script:** "I’m going to put your shirt on now." (pause)  
- **Action:** Bunch the shirt up and hold the neck hole open.  
- **Prompt:** "Where's Yumi's head?" (Slip it over her head). "Peek-a-boo! There she is!"  
- **Narrate:** "Now arm in... push, push! Good job." (Offer a micro-choice: "Do you want to hold your sock while I do this one?")

## 🔎 What to Observe This Week

Log brief notes (1–2 minutes daily):
- **Spatial Matching:** When she tries to put things into containers, is she getting less frustrated with the wider bowls? 
- **Communication:** When she points and says "aaahh" at a book, does she look back at your face to make sure you are looking too? (This is a huge leap in joint attention).
- **Mobility:** Is she letting go of the furniture to stand for longer than 10 seconds? 
- **Sleep:** Is the "fake crying" at bedtime continuing, or was it a one-off overtired night? 

---

## 🔁 End-of-Week Reflection / Decision Rules

Ask yourself at the end of the week:
- Did the wide-mouth containers help reduce her frustration with putting items back inside? (If yes → slowly introduce slightly smaller openings next week).
- Is she still struggling to rotate her wrists for puzzles? (If yes → keep rotational tasks paused for now; her fine motor skills will catch up soon!).
- Is she initiating the table-wiping unprompted now? 

---

## ➡️ Next Week Progression 

- **If Enveloping/In-and-Out is strong:** We will introduce nesting bowls (putting smaller bowls inside bigger bowls) to challenge her spatial reasoning safely.
- **If pointing/vocalizing increases:** We will start adding specific word modeling (e.g., when she says "aaahh" and points to a dog, you say "Dog. Woof!").
- **If she starts taking independent steps:** We will introduce a sturdy push-toy to help her transport items across the room.

---

## 🛟 Safety Reminders
- **Choking Risk:** Because she loves putting things in and out of containers, ensure all toys used for container play are much larger than a toilet paper tube. 
- **Boundary Testing:** Keep heavy items (like the Stanley tumblers) pushed far back on counters so she cannot pull them down on herself while cruising. Ensure the humidifier cord is completely out of reach.
- **Water/Wipes:** Supervise closely when she is wiping the table to ensure she doesn't try to chew or suck on the wet wipe. 
- **Climbing:** As she pulls to stand more confidently, ensure all tall furniture (bookshelves, dressers) is securely anchored to the wall.
